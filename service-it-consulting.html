What a vCIO does

A virtual CIO gives your leadership team executive-level technology guidance without the cost of a full-time hire. The work is strategic rather than hands-on: setting technology direction, building and defending a budget, overseeing vendors and contracts, and maintaining a roadmap that looks one to three years ahead. It is the difference between owning a plan and reacting to whatever lands on the desk this week.

Consulting and managed support, together

Consulting and day-to-day support answer different questions. Managed IT support keeps today running: the help desk, monitoring, and maintenance. Consulting looks further out, deciding where your systems should be in a few years, what to invest in, and in what order. The two work best together, and many organizations pair a support agreement with a vCIO so the daily operation and the long-term plan stay in step. Where regulation applies, we fold in compliance planning so requirements are met by design rather than scrambled for at audit time.

What a roadmap prevents

When technology decisions happen only after something breaks, spending becomes lumpy and expensive: emergency replacements at premium prices, licenses renewed without review, and security gaps discovered too late. A roadmap sequences hardware refreshes, software upgrades, and security investments over time, so costs are predictable and leadership can see what is coming instead of being surprised by it.

Aligning technology to growth

Technology should support where the business is going, not just where it has been. We help leadership connect their goals, whether that is adding a location, hiring, or meeting a new client requirement, to the systems that make them possible. This kind of planning serves growing regional organizations, including nonprofit organizations working within tight budgets and other organizations across Northern California that need their technology to keep pace with the work.

Frequently asked questions

What is a vCIO?

A vCIO, or virtual Chief Information Officer, gives your business executive-level technology leadership on a fractional basis. Instead of hiring a full-time CIO, you get strategic planning, budgeting guidance, and vendor oversight scaled to the size of your organization.

How is IT consulting different from managed IT support?

Managed support keeps your day-to-day technology running. Consulting looks further ahead: where your systems should be in one to three years, what to budget, which investments to prioritize, and how technology decisions support your business goals. Many clients use both together.

Do we really need a technology roadmap?

If technology decisions at your company happen reactively, usually when something breaks, a roadmap changes that. It sequences replacements, upgrades, and security investments over time, which smooths out spending and prevents emergency purchases at premium prices.

How does a consulting engagement start?

It starts with an assessment of your current environment: hardware age, software licensing, security posture, and how well your systems match the way your business actually works. From there we build recommendations and a prioritized plan you can act on.

How often do you meet with leadership?

The cadence is set to fit the engagement rather than a fixed contract term. Quarterly business reviews are common, giving leadership a regular point to check progress against the roadmap, review the budget, and adjust priorities. Some organizations meet more often during an active project or a busy stretch, and we scale the rhythm to what your team needs.
