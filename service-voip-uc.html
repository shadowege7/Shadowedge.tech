Frequently asked questions

What is VoIP and how is it different from a traditional phone system?

VoIP (Voice over IP) runs your phone service over your internet connection instead of dedicated phone lines. It typically costs less than legacy systems, scales by adding licenses instead of hardware, and lets your team answer business calls from desk phones, computers, or mobile apps.

Can we keep our existing business phone numbers?

Yes. Existing numbers can be ported to the new system, and we manage that process during the transition so callers never notice the change.

What do we need for good call quality?

Reliable internet bandwidth and a properly configured network. We assess your connection and set up quality-of-service rules so voice traffic gets priority over less time-sensitive data, which is what prevents choppy or dropped calls.

Does VoIP work for remote and hybrid teams?

Very well. Because the system lives in the cloud, employees can take and make business calls from anywhere using a softphone app or a desk phone at home, with the same extensions, voicemail, and call routing as in the office.
