Cloud and network engineering for dependable operations

We design and support modern infrastructure that improves resilience, performance, and connectivity across your organization.

From cloud migration planning to network optimization, our engineering services help businesses create more scalable, secure environments without unnecessary disruption.

  • Cloud architecture and migration planning
  • Network design and optimization
  • Secure connectivity for hybrid and remote teams

Cloud architecture and platform selection

The platform you run on shapes cost, security, and how easily your team works, so we start by matching the environment to your applications and workflow rather than to a favorite product. We design and support Microsoft 365, Google Workspace, and Amazon Web Services, and the right choice usually comes down to which line-of-business tools you rely on, your compliance requirements, and how your staff collaborate day to day.

Once a direction is set, we handle the build: tenant configuration, identity and access setup, licensing that fits your headcount, and the integration work that makes email, files, and applications behave as one environment. When data needs to move, we coordinate it with our migration and backup service so nothing is lost in the transition.

Network design for new builds and relocations

A new office or a move is the right moment to get the network foundation correct. We plan and build the physical and logical layout: structured cabling coordination with your contractors, firewall and switch configuration, wireless coverage that reaches every corner of the space, and site-to-site connectivity that links locations securely. Getting this right up front avoids the recurring problems that come from wiring and equipment chosen on the fly.

Design and build is where our work focuses. Once the environment is live, ongoing network management keeps it monitored, patched, and tuned over time.

Cloud, hybrid, or on-premises

Moving everything to the cloud is not automatically the right answer. Some applications run better locally, some carry compliance or bandwidth constraints, and budget matters. Many businesses settle on a hybrid model: email, files, and collaboration in the cloud, with specific systems kept on-premises. We assess your applications, connectivity, and requirements before recommending a direction, so the decision reflects how you operate rather than a trend.

Securing the environment from the start

We design identity-first, because in a cloud environment the login is the new perimeter. That means multifactor authentication, conditional access, and least-privilege permissions built in from day one, along with hardened configuration and monitoring, all coordinated with our cybersecurity practice. This approach serves professional services teams and offices across the Bay Area — from San Francisco and the Peninsula out to Livermore, Pleasanton, and Dublin — that need dependable, secure infrastructure without a dedicated engineering staff of their own.

Frequently asked questions

Which cloud platforms does ShadowEdge work with?

We design and support environments built on Microsoft 365, Google Workspace, and Amazon Web Services, and we hold partner relationships with each. The right platform depends on your applications, compliance requirements, and how your team works.

Should we move everything to the cloud or keep some systems on-premises?

It depends on your applications, bandwidth, compliance requirements, and budget. Many businesses land on a hybrid approach, moving email, files, and collaboration to the cloud while keeping specific line-of-business systems local. We assess your environment before recommending a direction.

Can you design the network for a new or relocated office?

Yes. We handle network design for new buildouts and relocations, including structured cabling coordination, firewall and switch configuration, wireless coverage planning, and secure connectivity back to your other locations or cloud resources.

How do you keep cloud environments secure?

Cloud security starts with identity: multifactor authentication, conditional access, and least-privilege permissions. We layer that with configuration hardening, monitoring, and backup, because cloud platforms secure their infrastructure but leave the configuration and data protection to you.

How long does a cloud migration take?

It depends on the volume of data, the number of users, and how many systems connect to what is moving. A single mailbox platform is quick; a full environment with shared files, line-of-business applications, and dependencies takes longer and is staged to limit disruption. We provide a realistic timeline after discovery, once we can see what actually needs to move.
