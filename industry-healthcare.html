Healthcare IT Support

ShadowEdge delivers HIPAA-aligned security controls, device management, and reliable support built for the operational demands of clinical and administrative teams.

Healthcare organizations operate under strict HIPAA requirements while supporting clinical workflows that cannot tolerate unplanned downtime. Patient records, scheduling systems, and diagnostic tools all depend on secure, available infrastructure, and a lapse in either security or uptime carries real consequences for patient care and regulatory standing. ShadowEdge works with medical practices, clinics, and administrative offices to build IT environments that protect PHI while keeping staff productive.

  • Security controls and documentation aligned with HIPAA Security Rule requirements
  • Managed device policies for workstations, mobile devices, and clinical equipment connected to the network
  • 24/7 monitoring and responsive support to minimize disruption to patient-facing systems
  • Encrypted backup and recovery planning for electronic health records and practice management data
  • Vendor coordination for EHR/EMR platforms and other clinical software

FAQ

Healthcare IT FAQs

How does ShadowEdge help with HIPAA compliance?

We implement the technical safeguards the HIPAA Security Rule requires, including access controls, encryption, audit logging, and secure backup, and we maintain the documentation that proves those controls exist. That evidence is what matters in an audit or after an incident.

How do you protect patient data (PHI)?

PHI protection is layered: encrypted storage and transmission, least-privilege access so staff only reach the records their role requires, multifactor authentication, endpoint protection on every device, and tested backups that ransomware cannot delete.

Do you work with our EHR and practice management vendors?

Yes. We coordinate directly with EHR/EMR and practice management vendors for updates, integrations, and troubleshooting, so your staff is not stuck in the middle relaying messages between the software company and IT.

What happens if our systems go down during clinic hours?

Our monitoring is designed to catch failing systems before they take the clinic down, and our support prioritizes patient-facing outages. Recovery planning, including tested backups of EHR and practice management data, determines how fast you are back up when hardware or ransomware strikes.
