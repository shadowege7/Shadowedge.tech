Legal IT FAQs

How do you protect attorney-client confidentiality in our systems?

Through encryption of data at rest and in transit, strict access controls so matter files are visible only to the people working them, multifactor authentication, and secure remote access for attorneys working outside the office.

Why are law firms targeted by ransomware and phishing?

Law firms concentrate exactly what attackers want: confidential client information, funds moving through trust accounts, and time pressure that makes firms more likely to pay to restore access quickly. That combination keeps legal services near the top of attacker target lists.

Do you support legal practice management and document systems?

Yes. We support and coordinate with vendors for practice management, document management, and time-and-billing platforms, handling updates, access, and integration issues so your staff can stay focused on casework.

What happens if our systems fail right before a filing deadline?

That scenario drives how we build legal environments: redundancy where it counts, monitoring that flags failing hardware early, and tested backups with known recovery times. Support requests tied to court deadlines are treated as urgent, not routine.
