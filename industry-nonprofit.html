Nonprofit IT Support

ShadowEdge provides cost-conscious managed IT and cybersecurity that helps mission-driven organizations do more with limited resources.

Nonprofit organizations often operate with lean staff and tight budgets, but still manage donor data, grant compliance requirements, and program systems that need to stay secure and available. Stretching IT further without adding risk requires a partner who understands both the constraints and the mission. ShadowEdge helps nonprofits build dependable, right-sized technology environments that support their work rather than compete with it for budget.

  • Security protections for donor data, financial records, and grant-related systems

FAQ

Nonprofit IT FAQs

How do you keep IT affordable for nonprofits?

We scope plans to what your organization actually needs, and we help you take advantage of the substantial nonprofit licensing discounts available from Microsoft, Google, and other vendors, which many organizations leave unclaimed.

With a limited budget, what should a nonprofit prioritize first?

Identity security first: multifactor authentication and basic access controls stop the most common attacks at minimal cost. Reliable backup comes next, then endpoint protection. We help sequence improvements so each dollar reduces real risk.

How do you protect donor and client data?

Donor records, case files, and payment information get encryption, access controls, and tested backups. A data breach at a nonprofit damages the community trust the mission depends on, so we treat this data with the same rigor as regulated industries.

Can you support a mix of staff, volunteers, and donated equipment?

Yes. Nonprofits often run varied environments with shared workstations and volunteer accounts. We bring device management and sensible account policies to that reality without imposing enterprise complexity your team does not need.
