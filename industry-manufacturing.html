Manufacturing IT Support

ShadowEdge provides network reliability, security, and technology planning for facilities with connected systems and distributed teams.

Manufacturing operations depend on connected equipment, production systems, and facility networks that need to stay online across shifts and locations. A network outage or security incident can halt production directly, making reliable infrastructure and layered security essential rather than optional. ShadowEdge works with manufacturers to design and manage networks that support both office operations and the systems running on the floor.

  • Network design and management for facilities with connected equipment and production systems
  • Security controls to protect operational systems alongside standard office IT
  • Monitoring and support to reduce unplanned downtime across shifts
  • Connectivity planning for multiple facilities or distributed teams
  • Backup and recovery planning for production and business data

FAQ

Manufacturing IT FAQs

Can you support the connected equipment on our production floor?

Yes. We design networks that keep production equipment connected and monitored while segmenting it from office systems, so a compromised workstation cannot reach the machines that run your operation.

How do you minimize IT-related production downtime?

Through proactive monitoring of the network and servers your production depends on, redundancy for the most critical links, and maintenance scheduled around production windows instead of during them.

Why is ransomware a particular threat to manufacturers?

Because downtime is immediately and visibly expensive, attackers assume manufacturers will pay quickly. Segmented networks, hardened access, and immutable backups take that leverage away and determine whether an incident is an inconvenience or a shutdown.

Can you connect our office, warehouse, and production facilities?

Yes. We build secure multi-site connectivity between offices, warehouses, and production floors with centralized management, so inventory systems, ERP access, and communications work consistently across every building.
